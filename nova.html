In March 2020 I was put on furlough from my workplace.


During the following months I decided that I wanted to learn how to make 3D models. So I began to teach myself

using online video and text tutorials.


Like most people, I began by learning how to create a doughnut and a coffee cup, but before long I wanted to design more adventurous models...

I started designing my own models by taking already existing products and adding a unique twist on them.


I only used "hard-body" modelling to create a super nintendo which looked like it came from the movie 'Tron'.

Whilst still working on "hard-body" modelling I began to experiment with "soft-body" work.

When creating this robot model I wanted to attempt to create soft fabric for the trousers and wrist strap.

It was during this time that I began to look into the posibility of 3D printing my models and the pitfalls that would occur if the model was not designed correctly.

I began learning how to correctly create a model in Blender using best practice, to ensure not only a solid model, but also one that can be printed without errors or failed prints.

Using what I learnt from the previous model I leaned more into the "soft-body" design when creating my models which lent itself to more realistic designs and real-world objects.

I currently host all my publicly avaiable 3d models on cults3d website, with some of my models gaining thousands of downloads and hundreds of likes.

I hope in the future to incorporate my 3D designs more into my websites on both a personal and professional level.
